---
title: "Animal inventory: track individual animals and batches"
description: "Manage livestock in LiteFarm by adding individual animals or batches, recording identifiers, origin, sex, and use, and assigning animal movement tasks."
---

LiteFarm's animal inventory lets you record every animal on your farm, either as an individual with its own profile or as a named batch representing a group. You can track type, breed, sex, origin, organic status, and more, and link animals to tasks like movement events. The inventory gives you a single place to view and manage all your livestock.

## Individual animals vs. animal batches

LiteFarm gives you two ways to record animals, depending on how much detail you need:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Individual animals" icon="paw">
    A record for a single animal with its own identifier, name, birth date, photo, and full profile. Use individual records when you need to track health history, pedigree, or per-animal details.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Animal batches" icon="layer-group">
    A record for a group of animals of the same type and breed. You record the count and optionally a sex breakdown. Use batches for poultry, fish, or other livestock where tracking individuals is not practical.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  You can mix individual animal records and batches for the same animal type. For example, you might have three named dairy cows as individuals and a batch of 50 broiler chickens.
</Note>

## Animal types and breeds

LiteFarm includes a set of default animal types and breeds. If your animals are not covered by the defaults, you can create custom types and breeds.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Default animal types">
    LiteFarm's built-in animal types cover common livestock categories. When you select a default type, a corresponding list of default breeds becomes available for selection.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Custom animal types">
    If your animal type is not in the default list, create a custom type by typing a new name in the type field. Custom types are saved to your farm and available for all future animals.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Custom breeds">
    Within any animal type (default or custom), you can type a new breed name to create a custom breed. Custom breeds are linked to their parent type.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Adding animals

The add animals flow is a multi-step form. You can add one or more animals or batches in a single session.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start the add flow">
    From the **Animals** section, tap **Add animals**. The flow begins with the basics step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set type, breed, and sex">
    Select the animal type (required). Optionally select a breed. For the sex, you can choose male, female, or provide a breakdown if adding a batch with mixed sexes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the count">
    For individual animals, the count is one. For batches, enter the total number of animals in the group. You can also choose to **Create individual profiles** if you want a separate record for each animal in the group.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the group (optional)">
    Optionally give the batch a name (for example, "Spring 2025 lambs") to make it easier to find in the inventory.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add details for each animal">
    For each animal or batch, fill in the details tab:

    - **Identifier** — a tag number, ear tag, microchip, or other ID. Select the identifier type and optionally record the tag colour.
    - **Use** — the intended use of the animal (for example, meat, dairy, breeding, or fibre).
    - **Origin** — whether the animal was born on the farm or brought in from outside. If brought in, you can record the supplier, purchase date, and price.
    - **Date of birth** — the animal's birth or hatch date.
    - **Weaning date** — for animals that are weaned.
    - **Organic status** — Non-Organic, Transitional, or Organic.
    - **Notes** — any free-text notes about the animal.
    - **Photo** — upload a photo of the animal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and save">
    Review the summary and tap **Save** to add the animals to your inventory.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Pedigree information (dam and sire names) is available in the details for individual animals if you need to track parentage.
</Tip>

## Animal identifier types

When recording an identifier for an individual animal, you can specify the type of identifier:

- Tag (ear tag, neck tag, or leg band)
- Microchip
- Brand
- Other (with a free-text description)

You can also record the colour of a tag to help visually identify animals in the field.

## Viewing the animal inventory

The animal inventory page shows all current animals and batches on your farm. From the inventory you can:

- **Search** by animal name, identifier, or batch name.
- **Filter** by animal type, breed, sex, location, task status, or origin using the filter panel.
- **Sort** the list to find animals quickly.
- **Select multiple animals** to perform bulk actions such as assigning a movement task or removing animals.

The inventory also shows key performance indicators (KPIs) at the top of the page — a count of total animals, active animals, and other summary metrics depending on your filter selection.

## Animal groups and batch management

While LiteFarm does not have a dedicated "group" entity separate from batches, you can organise animals by giving batches descriptive names and using the filter tools to narrow the inventory by type, location, or other attributes.

When adding animals, the option to create individual profiles from a count lets you quickly generate records for a whole group and then customise each one.

## Animal movement tasks

To record moving animals from one location to another, create an **Animal movement task**:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new task">
    From the Tasks page or from the animal inventory, create a new task and select **Animal movement** as the task type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select animals">
    Choose the individual animals or batches being moved. You can select multiple animals at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the destination">
    Select the farm location the animals are moving to. This updates the animal's recorded location when the task is completed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the date and assignee">
    Pick the movement date and assign the task to the worker carrying it out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the task">
    When the movement is done, mark the task complete. The animals' location in the inventory updates automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Removing animals

When an animal leaves the farm (through sale, death, or other removal), you can mark it as removed rather than deleting the record. Select the animal in the inventory, choose **Remove**, and enter a reason and date. Removed animals are hidden from the active inventory by default but can be shown by toggling the **Show removed** filter.
