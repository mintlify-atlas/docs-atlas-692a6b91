Insights gives you a set of evidence-based metrics that help you understand how your farm is performing — ecologically, economically, and socially. Each insight draws on data you have already logged in LiteFarm, so the more you record, the richer the picture becomes. Because LiteFarm is a not-for-profit platform developed with researchers, the data you contribute (anonymously and with your consent) also supports ongoing agricultural science.

## Opening insights

Navigate to **Insights** from the main menu. The overview screen shows all available insights with a summary value for each. Tap any card to open that insight in detail.

<Note>
The TAPE survey and some insights require an internet connection and are only available to Farm Owners and Farm Managers.
</Note>

## Biodiversity

Your biodiversity score shows the variety of crops currently growing across your fields. A more diverse mix of plants supports pest suppression, pollinator habitat, and soil health.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="What it measures" icon="leaf">
    The number and relative proportion of distinct crop species planted on your farm. Results are shown as a species list with percentage share of planted area.
  </Card>
  <Card title="How to improve it" icon="arrow-up">
    Add more crop varieties in **Crops**, plan intercrops or cover crops in **Management plans**, and map each planting area on your **Farm map**.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Tap **Biodiversity** on the Insights screen to calculate your current score. The calculation runs against your live planting data and may take a moment to load. Scores are cached for 30 minutes so you can navigate away and return without waiting.

<Tip>
Even small changes count. Adding a single new crop variety to a field increases species richness and moves your score upward.
</Tip>

## Soil organic matter

Soil organic matter (SOM) is one of the best indicators of long-term soil health. Higher SOM means better water retention, increased nutrient availability, and greater resilience to drought.

Your SOM insight displays the most recently logged organic matter percentage for each field on your farm. Values are drawn from soil sample tasks you have completed and logged in LiteFarm.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Record a soil sample task">
    Go to **Tasks**, create a **Soil sample** task, assign it to the relevant field, and mark it complete when you have your lab results.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the organic matter result">
    When completing the task, enter the percentage organic matter value from your soil report.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View your SOM insight">
    Open **Insights → Soil organic matter** to see the current percentage per field alongside a relative bar showing each field's share of total farm SOM.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The preview percentage shown on the Insights overview is the average SOM across all sampled fields.

## Labour happiness

The labour happiness insight tracks how workers feel about the tasks they complete. After a worker marks a task as done, they are invited to rate their mood on a 1–5 scale. These ratings are averaged by task type so you can see which activities your team finds most and least rewarding.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Ratings scale" icon="star">
    Workers rate each completed task from 1 (very unhappy) to 5 (very happy). The average is shown per task type on the insight screen.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Why it matters" icon="users">
    Understanding which tasks score lower helps you identify areas for process improvement, schedule relief, or training — supporting worker wellbeing and retention.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The overview screen shows a single preview score (for example, `3.4/5`) representing the overall average. If no mood data has been logged yet, the insight shows **Unavailable** until workers begin rating tasks.

<Note>
Mood ratings are voluntary. Workers can complete a task without submitting a rating.
</Note>

## Prices

The prices insight compares your recorded sale prices against what other LiteFarm farms in your region are selling the same crops for. This helps you gauge whether your prices are competitive and spot trends in local markets.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set your search radius">
    Open **Insights → Prices** and use the distance selector to choose how far out to search: 5, 10, 25, or 50 km (or the equivalent in miles if your farm uses imperial units).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the chart">
    Each crop you have sold appears with a chart comparing your average sale price to the regional average. The insight also shows how many nearby farms contributed data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log more sales">
    Prices are drawn from the **Finances** section. The more sales you record, the more accurately your average is represented.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
The prices feature requires your farm to have a map location set. If you haven't added your farm's address yet, go to **Farm settings → Basic profile** and enter your location.
</Info>

The overview shows your most recent price as a percentage of the regional market average — for example, `112% of market`.

## TAPE survey

TAPE stands for **Tool for Agroecology Performance Evaluation**. It is a structured self-assessment developed by the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) that measures your farm across ten dimensions of agroecological performance.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="The ten dimensions" icon="chart-radar">
    Diversity, Synergy, Recycling, Efficiency, Resilience, Culture and food traditions, Co-creation and sharing of knowledge, Human and social values, Circular economy and solidarity, Responsible governance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="After completion" icon="chart-bar">
    Your results are displayed as a radar chart with a score from 0–100 for each dimension. You can review them any time from **Insights → TAPE**.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the TAPE survey">
    Tap **TAPE** on the Insights screen. The survey is available to Farm Owners and Managers who are online. A country-specific version may be shown depending on your farm's location.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the questionnaire">
    Work through each page of the survey. Your progress is saved automatically so you can pause and return without losing answers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit and view results">
    Click **Complete** on the final page. Your radar chart is generated immediately and stored against your farm.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You only need to complete the TAPE survey once. After submission, the Insights screen shows **Completed** for the TAPE card and you can return to your results at any time.
</Tip>

## Insights and research

LiteFarm is developed in partnership with researchers at the University of British Columbia and other institutions. With your consent, anonymised farm data — including insights metrics — may be shared with research partners to improve agricultural sustainability tools. You can review and manage your data-sharing preferences in **Account settings**.
