The farm map is the spatial hub of your LiteFarm account. It gives you a satellite or road-view map centred on your farm where you can draw all your locations — fields, greenhouses, barns, watercourses, and more — and then see which crops are planted there and which tasks are due. Everything else in LiteFarm, from management plans to task assignments, connects back to the locations you define on the map.

## Location types

LiteFarm supports three categories of location: areas (polygon shapes), lines, and points. Each type captures a different kind of feature on your farm.

## Drawing a new location

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the map">
    Navigate to **Map** in the main menu. The map opens centred on your farm's grid point.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the add drawer">
    Tap the **+** button at the bottom of the map. A drawer slides up showing all available location types. This button is only visible to farm admins and managers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a location type">
    Tap the location type you want to add (for example, **Field** or **Greenhouse**). A drawing toolbar appears at the top of the screen and the map enters drawing mode.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Draw the shape">
    - **Area**: Tap each corner of the location on the map to place vertices. A polygon forms as you add points.
    - **Line**: Tap along the route of the feature. Each tap adds a node.
    - **Point**: Tap once on the map to place the pin.

    For buffer zones and watercourses, a panel appears asking you to set the width before you confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the drawing">
    When you are satisfied with the shape, tap **Confirm**. If you want to try again, tap **Try again** to clear the drawing and start over, or tap the back arrow to cancel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in details and save">
    A form opens asking for the location's name and any type-specific details (such as organic history for fields). Fill in the required fields and tap **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The map requires an internet connection to load satellite imagery. If you are offline, drawing mode is disabled.
</Note>

<Tip>
  First-time users see a spotlight tutorial when they start drawing an area or line. You can dismiss it if you already know the workflow.
</Tip>

## Editing and deleting locations

To edit or delete an existing location, tap it on the map. A panel opens showing the location's details. From there you can:

- **Edit details** — update the name, notes, organic history, or other type-specific fields by tapping the pencil icon or the **Edit** button.
- **Redraw** — change the shape of the location by selecting **Redraw** and adjusting the vertices or path.
- **Delete** — remove the location from your farm. LiteFarm checks whether the location has active management plans or incomplete tasks before allowing deletion. If dependencies exist, you will be prompted to resolve them first.

<Warning>
  Deleting a location permanently removes it and its associated data. This action cannot be undone.
</Warning>

## Viewing crops and tasks on the map

The map does more than show location boundaries — it also surfaces what is happening in each location:

- **Crop overlays** — fields, gardens, and greenhouses with active management plans display a crop indicator. Tap the location to see which varieties are planted and their current growth stage.
- **Task pins** — locations with upcoming or overdue tasks show a task indicator. Tap to see a summary of pending work.
- **Filter** — tap the filter icon in the map toolbar to show or hide specific location types, making it easier to focus on the part of your farm you are working in.

## Exporting the map

Farm owners and managers can export a screenshot of the current map view. Tap the export icon in the toolbar and choose to either **Download** the image directly or **Share** it by email.
