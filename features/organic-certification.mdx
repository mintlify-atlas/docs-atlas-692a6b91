---
title: 'Organic certification: prepare and export your records'
description: 'Set up your certifier, define your reporting period, and export certification-ready documents including management plans and product records.'
---

LiteFarm helps farms pursuing organic certification stay organised throughout the year by collecting the information certifiers need in one place. When it is time to submit your annual documentation, you can generate an export that packages your management plans, products used, field organic status, and other records into a set of files ready to send to your certifying body.

<Note>
Organic certification support is available to Farm Owners and Farm Managers. Farm Workers and Extension Officers cannot modify certification settings or trigger exports.
</Note>

---

## Setting up your certification

Before you can export certification documents, you need to tell LiteFarm that you are pursuing certification and select your certifying body.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Certifications">
    Navigate to **Certifications** from the main menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Indicate you are pursuing certification">
    On the certifier survey screen, confirm that your farm is currently seeking or maintaining organic certification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your certification type">
    Choose your certification from the list of supported certification programs. Available options are filtered to those recognised in your country.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your certifying body">
    Choose your certifier from the list. The certifiers shown are filtered to those that operate in your country. If you cannot find your certifier, select **Other** and enter your certifier's name manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your selections">
    Click **Save** to record your certifier information. You can update this at any time if your certification status or certifier changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Certification survey questions

The certifier survey collects key facts about your certification status. The questions cover:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Certification interest">
    Whether your farm is actively pursuing or maintaining an organic certification. If you answer no, the export feature is not available.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Certification program">
    The name of the certification scheme you are working under (for example, USDA Organic, Canada Organic, or another national standard).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Certifying body">
    The specific organisation that will review and approve your certification application. LiteFarm maintains a database of certifiers by country and matches options to your farm's location.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Setting a reporting period

When you are ready to prepare your export, you set the date range that your certification documentation should cover. This is usually the previous 12-month period, but your certifier may specify different dates.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the reporting period screen">
    From the **Certifications** section, proceed to **Reporting period** (or continue from the certifier survey if you are setting up for the first time).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter start and end dates">
    Enter the **From date** and **To date** that define your certification reporting period. Only records falling within this range are included in the export.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provide your email address">
    Confirm the email address where the export files should be sent. This defaults to your LiteFarm account email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Continue to the survey">
    Click **Continue** to proceed to the certifier survey (or to the export step if you have already completed the survey).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Exporting certification documents

Once your certifier and reporting period are set, you can generate your export.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the certification survey page">
    Navigate to **Certifications → Survey**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the summary">
    Check that the certifier, certification type, and reporting period shown are correct before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request the export">
    Click **Export**. LiteFarm queues your export for processing and sends the files to your email address when ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Export processing can take a few minutes. You will receive an email with a download link once the files are ready. You do not need to stay on the certifications screen while you wait.
</Info>

## What is included in the export

The export packages all the records your certifier needs to review your organic status for the reporting period.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Seed and crop variety records" icon="sprout">
    Crop varieties planted during the reporting period, including supplier, organic seed status, whether seeds were treated, and whether genetically engineered varieties were used.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Input products used on crops" icon="flask">
    Soil amendments and pest control products applied to organic or transitional fields, including product name, supplier, quantity, date used, and whether the product is on the permitted substances list.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cleaning products used" icon="droplets">
    Cleaning and sanitation products applied to non-crop-enabled locations (barns, equipment, and so on), with the same detail as crop inputs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Field and location records" icon="map">
    Each mapped location on your farm with its area, crops grown, and organic status (organic, transitional, non-organic, or non-producing) as at the end of the reporting period.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Documents uploaded to the **Documents** section of your farm that are valid during the reporting period (for example, input product approvals or prior certification letters) are also attached to the export as supporting files.

## Organic status of fields

Your export reflects the organic history of each field, garden, and greenhouse on your farm. LiteFarm tracks status changes over time, so if a field changed from transitional to organic mid-year, the export correctly represents each period.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Organic">
    The field has been managed under organic practices for the full required transition period and has not had any non-permitted inputs applied during the reporting period.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Transitional">
    The field is in the process of converting to organic. It has not yet completed the required transition period.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Non-organic">
    The field is not managed under organic practices, either by choice or because a non-permitted input was applied.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Non-producing">
    The location is not a crop-growing area (for example, a barn, buffer zone, or ceremonial area) and is reported as non-producing.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

To update the organic status of a field, go to **Farm map**, select the location, and edit its organic history.

<Warning>
Organic status is based on your logged history. If you have not recorded status changes in LiteFarm, the export may not accurately reflect your field's certification status. Keep your location records up to date throughout the year.
</Warning>
