LiteFarm sends notifications to keep you informed about tasks and farm activity. Notifications appear in the **Notifications** section of the app, accessible from the bell icon in the navigation bar. You can also search and filter your notifications from that screen.

---

## Notification types

LiteFarm currently sends two types of automated notifications.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Daily tasks due today" icon="calendar-check">
    Sent each morning to alert you and your team that tasks are due on the current day. This helps everyone prioritise their work without having to check the task list manually.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Weekly unassigned tasks" icon="calendar-clock">
    Sent once a week to Farm Owners and Managers listing tasks that have no assignee. This prompts you to assign outstanding work before it falls overdue.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
Notifications are sent based on the task due dates you set when creating or editing tasks. Keeping due dates accurate ensures notifications are timely and relevant.
</Note>

---

## Viewing notifications

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the notifications screen">
    Tap the bell icon in the navigation bar, or navigate to **Notifications** from the main menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse your notifications">
    Notifications are listed in reverse chronological order. Each card shows the notification title, a short description, and the time it was received.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search or filter">
    Use the search bar at the top of the screen to filter notifications by keyword. This is useful on farms with a high volume of activity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the related record">
    Tap a notification card to mark it as read and navigate directly to the referenced task or record.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## How notification delivery works

Notifications are delivered within the LiteFarm app. When new notifications arrive that you have not yet seen, an alert indicator appears on the bell icon in the navigation bar.

When you open the notifications screen, all currently unread notifications are marked as read and the alert indicator is cleared.

<Info>
LiteFarm uses a real-time subscription mechanism to push notifications to your device. If you are using LiteFarm in a browser, you may need to keep the tab open or return to the app to receive new notifications.
</Info>

## Notification preferences

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I turn off specific notification types?">
    Notification preferences are managed at the application level. If you want to reduce notification volume, ensure that tasks have realistic due dates so daily alerts are only triggered when there is genuinely work to do that day.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who receives daily task notifications?">
    Daily task notifications are sent to the assigned worker for each task. Farm Owners and Managers also receive a summary of all tasks due each day across the farm.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who receives weekly unassigned task notifications?">
    Weekly summaries of unassigned tasks are sent to Farm Owners and Farm Managers only. Farm Workers do not receive this notification type.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if I have no tasks due?">
    If no tasks are due on a given day, no daily notification is sent. The weekly unassigned tasks notification is only sent if there are unassigned tasks in the upcoming week.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tips for effective notifications

<Tip>
Assign every task to a specific team member. Tasks without an assignee only appear in the weekly manager summary, meaning the relevant worker may not be alerted in time.
</Tip>

<Tip>
Review your task due dates at the start of each season. Outdated due dates from previous seasons can generate misleading daily alerts.
</Tip>

<Tip>
Use the search bar in Notifications to find alerts related to a specific crop, field, or task type when you have many notifications to review.
</Tip>
